This page provides a paraphrased summary of Web Sustainability Guidelines (WSG). For the full document, see https://www.w3.org/TR/web-sustainability-guidelines/.

It aims to be a helpful entry point for individuals and affected parties who have an interest in digital sustainability and the work undertaken by the Sustainable Web Interest Group.

While this document does provide a quick summary of numerous key aspects of the WSG specification, it must be established that it in no way should be used as a replacement for following the guidelines and success criteria as laid out in the complete, long-form specification. Reasons for this include that this summary is neither intended to be in-depth nor feature-complete. Therefore, its role as an overview to help orientate affected parties to the specification's purpose in no way provides conformance to Web Sustainability criteria.

Introduction

Context

The digital industry is now responsible for between 2-5% of global emissions [[ICT-IMPACT]] [[EUC]] [[8BT]] [[UN]] [[WB]], more than that of the aviation industry. Since the Paris Agreement, average web page sizes have increased by over 100% on desktop and 180% on mobile [[PAGE-WEIGHT]]. Between 2015 and 2021, internet users increased by 60%, whilst internet traffic increased by 440% [[DATA]]. In essence, if the Internet were a country it would be one of the top five polluters. [[COUNTRY]]

Summary

Web Sustainability Guidelines (WSG) explains how to design and implement digital products and services that put the planet, people, and prosperity first. The guidelines are best practices based on measurable, evidence-based research. The guidelines are in line with both the Sustainable Web Manifesto [[MANIFESTO]] and Manifesto for a Humane Web [[HUMANE]].

Highlights

Knowing where to start, especially with a document as long and dense as WSG is quite a challenge. You may wish to scan through a supplement such as our quick reference which provides a checklist-based overview of every guideline. Or if you just want a couple of important tasks you can dive right into them. If so, consider the below as a few of our highlights (though please note, that this does not reduce the importance of the others).

Show / Hide highlights by category.

The below will have a high impact on your product or service's digital sustainability:

User Experience Design
  • Understand user requirements or constraints, resolving barriers to access
  • Minimize non-essential content, interactivity, or journeys
  • Regularly test and maintain compatibility
Web Development
  • Give third parties the same priority as first parties during assessment
  • Use dependencies appropriately and ensure maintenance
  • Use the most efficient solution for your service
Hosting, Infrastructure, and Systems
  • Use sustainable hosting
  • Optimize caching and support offline access
  • Reduce data transfer with compression
Business Strategy and Product Management
  • Have an ethical and sustainable product strategy
  • Calculate the environmental impact
  • Implement continuous improvement procedures

Overview

The guidelines should be seen as a starting point in a sustainability journey; they cover the following:

User Experience Design

Covering research and ideation, journey design, content and assets, and quality assurance.

Show / Hide available guidance.

In relation to research and ideation, make sure you:

  • Map all areas where your digital product could impact the planet, people, and prosperity.
  • Assess all affected party issues during ideation as well as user needs; then define appropriate test policies.

In relation to journey design, make sure you:

  • Create a lightweight experience, using a design system for interface consistency that ensure navigation and way-finding are well-structured, the user's attention is respected and users are not manipulated.
  • Implement forms that are usable, accessible, and minimal and assist users with errors.
  • Only implement useful notifications that improve the user's experience.

In relation to content and assets, make sure you:

  • Take a sustainable approach to media, animations, typefaces, and documents.
  • Provide alternatives to visual and audio content.
  • Provide clear, inclusive content with purpose.

In relation to quality assurance, make sure you:

  • Have open working and documentation processes such as design systems.
  • Conduct regular audits and testing, including value, usability and compatibility testing into releases, as well as using inputs from user journey tracking to identify issues/opportunities.

Web Development

Covering development approach, code minimization, code coherence, and code security.

Show / Hide available guidance.

In relation to the development approach, make sure you:

  • Identify indicators and metrics that will encourage sustainable development approaches, and use sustainability as a success criterion of your technical requirements.

In relation to code minimization, make sure you:

  • Focus on removing bottlenecks and reducing compute load on a user's device by removing rendering blockages, using code splitting, managing dependencies rigorously assessing third-party code, services, and minifying everything.
  • Hunt out valueless code by tree shaking your code, avoiding duplication, fewer and simpler queries, and (sparingly) using the latest JavaScript and APIs.

In relation to code coherence, make sure you:

  • Enable other tools to interpret your code by using HTML and metadata correctly enabling code-based way-finding, avoiding deprecated or proprietary code, providing expected files, and using the latest standards in relation to website assets.
  • Facilitate your user's low-carbon choices by using a device-adaptable layout, adapting to user preferences.

In relation to code security, make sure you:

  • Use the latest stable version of your chosen code language and regularly ensure your scripts are secure.

Hosting, Infrastructure, and Systems

Covering environment commissioning, minimizing environment and data, and minimizing human disruption.

Show / Hide available guidance.

Make sure you:

  • Commission environments by selecting a sustainable service provider, ensuring support of asynchronous processing, using the lowest appropriate infrastructure tier, and minimizing the use of additional environments.
  • Minimize environments and reduce data transfer by compressing files, managing duplicate data, storing data according to user needs, optimizing caching, refreshing data only when the user needs it, and using CDNs and edge caching.
  • Reduce human disruption by automating where appropriate and dealing with errors and redirects.

Business Strategy and Product Management

Covering reporting, disclosure, strategy, and policies from both an organizational and project level.

Show / Hide available guidance.

In relation to organizational level reporting and disclosure, make sure you:

  • Assign responsibility for sustainability as a core part of people's roles.
  • Define clear sustainability goals and metrics, which are verified and upon which you report regularly.

In relation to organizational level strategy and policies, make sure you:

  • Have business models based on positive impact, which actively listen to all affected parties, and ensure economic benefits are shared with employees, suppliers, and wider society.
  • Define and implement policies for Justice, Equity, Diversity and Inclusion, ethical and responsible finance/investments, philanthropy, working with suppliers who align with your values, reduction of e-waste, data privacy, appropriate data management, and considered use of emerging technologies.
  • Fully upskill affected parties on all of the above.

In relation to product or website-level strategy policies, make sure you:

  • Establish if the product or website is needed; if it is, then, design an ethical and sustainable product strategy to guide it, define its requirements and estimate its emissions, put budgets around those emissions, and communicate to users the impact their decisions will have.
  • Once live, follow a product management and maintenance strategy to keep efficient, continuously improve it, openly and honestly communicate the changes you have made, and plan for its end of life.
  • Use open source software where possible.
