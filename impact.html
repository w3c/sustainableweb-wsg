This document describes the impact ratings provided alongside Web Sustainability Guidelines (WSG). The ratings and timeframes support prioritisation of guidelines based on expected impact and when that impact is likely to occur across people, planet, and prosperity.

Help improve this page by sharing your ideas, suggestions, or comments via GitHub issues.

To provide feedback regarding this document, the preferred method is using GitHub. It is free to create a GitHub account to file issues. A list of issues filed as well as archives of previous mailing list public-sustainableweb@w3.org (archive) discussions are publicly available.

Introduction

This document provides "high", "medium", "low", and "indeterminate" impact ratings for each guideline within Web Sustainability Guidelines (WSG) across the spectrum of people, planet, and prosperity. These ratings are accompanied by an indicator ("short", "medium", and "long") reflecting the timeframe in which the impact is likely realized.

These factors go into calculating an "impact score" for each guideline. This score provides a means for adopters of the WSG to assess the impact of each guideline and prioritise the guidelines they may choose to act upon.

Each guideline has an explanation of the rationale behind each set of ratings and timeframe because some guidelines in the WSG are not measurable using a clear metric or tool. As a result, for some guidelines, we have used available research and input from industry experts in order to provide ratings and impact timeframes.

Where appropriate, we included the suitable or recommended metrics that can be used to assess the impact of a given guideline.

Impact ratings and scores may be used to support sustainability reporting and assessment. They can help organizations describe and compare the relative impact of guidelines over time. They may also be referenced alongside established reporting frameworks, such as the Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) where relevant.

Impact ratings

Indeterminate
The impact of a guideline on a given category cannot be easily assessed.
Low
The guideline has a small positive impact on the given category.
Medium
The guideline has a moderate positive impact on the given category.
High
The guideline has a significant positive impact on the given category.

Timeframe

Short
The impact of the guideline will be noticed quickly (e.g. within the next month, the next sprint etc.)
Medium
The impact of the guideline will be noticed after some time (e.g. after a few months, after the next major release etc.)
Long
The impact of the guideline will be noticed after a lengthy period (e.g. after a year, after several major releases etc.)

Categories

Planet
This guideline prioritizes the health of the environment, ecosystems, and the planet overall.
People
This guideline prioritizes giving access to resources, information, and opportunities necessary for their well-being.
Prosperity
This guideline prioritizes financial stability and equitable economic development, usually via the circular economy.

Impact score

The impact score is derived from the impact ratings and impact timeframe for each guideline. Higher values mean the guideline has a higher impact in a shorter period of time, while lower values mean the opposite.

Impact score calculation

Points 0 1 2 3 3 2 1

Each category (Planet, People, Prosperity) is assigned an impact rating and timeframe based on the table below. These are then summed to calculate the impact score.

Assumptions

Impact ratings are provided at the guideline level. Future versions may offer ratings at the Success Criteria level. Assume that each success criteria under a parent guideline shares the same impact ratings and timeframes as that guideline.

Impact ratings are for individual guidelines. Ratings are not compounded.
